# Enterprise Billing

The Enterprise plan primarily uses a **per-seat** billing model, tailored for enterprise-level teams, cross-department collaboration, high-security auditing, and complex multi-person R&D scenarios.

Enterprise Standard seats start at a minimum of `5` seats by default, and include a higher tier of permission controls, full-chain audit logging, and centralized billing management.

## Enterprise Plans & Billing Rules

Enterprise plans are divided into the following three tiers based on organization size and technical depth:

> **Note:** Enterprise Max's per-seat price is intentionally lower than Enterprise Standard. Max includes everything in Standard and adds a higher compute multiplier and a dedicated support channel — the lower per-seat rate reflects its higher-volume commitment (6 seats minimum, by application), not a reduced feature set.

## Enterprise Overage Billing Mechanism

Each Enterprise seat includes a high-spec base compute pack and service allowance. Once actual team usage exceeds the default range included in the plan, the excess is billed for actual consumption as follows:

- **Model call consumption**: Uniformly metered and audited via the enterprise-grade model gateway CodeGW, with usage beyond the base allowance billed on a tiered basis by actual token throughput.
- **Agent resource consumption**: Compute resources generated by Sub Agents or Swarms deployed within the enterprise while executing large-scale task graphs are dynamically deducted at a fixed points/hour rate — Swarm deployments are billed at **150 points/hour**, and Sub (single Agent) deployments are billed at **10 points/hour**.

## BYOK (Bring Your Own Key)

The Enterprise plan fully supports **BYOK** mode.

Enterprise customers can seamlessly connect their own, controlled model service keys (for example, their own OpenAI, Anthropic Claude, Google Gemini, or enterprise-dedicated model API keys).

- **BYOK cost attribution**: When BYOK is enabled, token costs from underlying model calls are billed directly to the enterprise's own provider account, reducing platform pass-through costs.
- **Platform management fees**: Even when using the enterprise's own key, core platform-side functionality — Agent architecture orchestration, the resource provisioning wizard (Resource Grant), the team's high-risk operation approval firewall, full-chain auditing, and the management dashboard — continues to be billed or deducted as base platform resource usage according to the Enterprise plan's seat and platform rules.

> **Note**: BYOK is not an Enterprise-exclusive capability — the personal plan's **Free** and **Plus** tiers also support connecting your own key for full feature access. What Enterprise adds on top of BYOK is team-level resource orchestration, the approval firewall, and full-chain auditing management capabilities — not BYOK itself.
